Beloved Boston vegetarian victuals chain Clover is [closing today](https://www.bostonglobe.com/2026/05/26/business/clover-closing-restaurants-prices-jobs/) and I’m bummed. I love their food – the chickpea fritter, meatball sub, Japanese sweet potato sandwich, rosemary fries, and more were all bright and layered and made vegetarian fast food a delicious prospect instead of punishment.

We got their food boxes delivered once in a while and the portions were massive, the menu always fun and varied. I dream of the apple-themed box we got a few years ago and the divine ploughman’s sandwich therein: apple chutney, cheese, aioli, and arugula on a baguette that we ate outside in the creeping fall chill. Baking their pita from frozen was the shortest path to heaven.

One of Clover's best selling points was the largely local ingredients; Clover’s chief executive says those ingredients “cost 30 to 50 percent more than two years ago”, and even though locations were making a profit you can’t fight those numbers. An “8 percent, on average” increase in sales can’t beat the muddy hillside it feels like we’re slipping down.

Another local business, even more niche than vegetarian feel-good food, is closing down. Dover Saddlery, purveyor of equestrian bits and bobs, has been around for 50 years and is shutting everything down due to similar "inflationary pressures". Emily wasn’t their target audience, but it was one of a handful of places to get horsey equipment; now there’s two.

It’s true that things change and restaurants close all the time, but this feels part of a bigger picture being painted and I don’t like how it’s turning out. [Everything is getting more expensive](https://apnews.com/article/us-inflation-consumer-iran-war-3f11b7fdd20ea56d2f0895e5241af7b6) and people weren’t doing great before that. If there's nowhere left nearby and it costs too much to go anywhere, I suppose the ideal outcome for ghouls is that you stay in your apartment (you can't afford a house) and become a *Matrix*-esque human battery but instead you watch ads. If you get sick you can flush yourself down the goo toilet and save the ambulance the trip from ten towns over.

I'm getting increasingly dramatic, so maybe it's time for lunch. I'm headed to Westford for one last heavenly sandwich to mask the bitter taste in my mouth.
