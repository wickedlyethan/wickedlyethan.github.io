I really didn’t like [King’s comic](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/59854151-supergirl). I found it pretentious, dour, and brutally overwritten in a literal way - the art was covered up with words, words, words.

Choosing that story, one in which Kara is world-weary and ground down by an uncaring universe, as the first cinematic attempt since the unexplainable [1984 debacle](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0088206/) is baffling.

Then again, this didn’t turn out much better! This movie doesn’t nail any of the basics; scenes barely even happen so much as they dribble out like vomit. Sometimes they blurt out with a drunken exclamation – it becomes a predictably loud movie – but it feels like you missed pages of bad but badly needed dialog. Who is Supergirl? Where are we? When are we? What the fuck is going on?

Gunn’s comic book-y (derogatory) hand-waiving shorthand for “the universe is as big as I say it is” strained credulity in last year’s more focused, better movie, but here it’s undisciplined and uninteresting. It’s pop culture shorthand on fumes.

I’m sick of fake alien languages in movies. Have characters speak in English, no one cares how. Every line in some nonsense subtitle is instantly forgettable, unquotable, and hamstrings the performers. The alleged emotional core of this thing is flashbacks that sound like *Animal Crossing* trying to do *Hamlet*. The rest is Minimum Viable Dialog.

The frustratingly self-evident but hard to articulate part is that Supergirl as a character is simultaneously stupid and wholly worthy of great stories. This ain’t it, neither was *Woman of Tomorrow*. What does the [“Batgirl of Burnside”](https://dc.fandom.com/wiki/Batgirl:_Batgirl_of_Burnside) version of this look like? What if Supergirl was the woman of *today*?

At the very least, remember the ABCs of making a fucking movie.
