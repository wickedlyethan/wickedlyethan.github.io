Last night Emily and I saw the [Kendall Square orchestra](https://www.kendallsquareorchestra.org/) perform Gustav Mahler’s *[Symphony No. 6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symphony_No._6_(Mahler))* (1903–1904) at Harvard’s Sanders Theater. The wooden hall was filled with dizzying drama rapidly changing from march to elegy and back again, a 100-piece orchestra at times struggling to keep up with the pointedly chaotic symphony. The conductor was dynamic and charming, not at all a stuffy academic in tuxedo tails; he hopped, shrugged, beckoned, and pointed commandingly at single players to summon the imposing music from the beginning of the end of the Austro-Hungarian Empire.

As the fourth movement – the longest, loudest, and most “controversial” due to the (underwhelming) inclusion of a [giant wooden hammer](https://cso.org/experience/article/9839/dropping-the-hammer-in-mahlers-sixth-symphony) as an instrument – wrapped, I gently returned from hypnosis. Even if the piece wasn’t always for me, what a privilege and joy to see it performed.

Mahler’s sixth, we learned from a [startlingly lengthy essay](https://classical-scene.com/2026/02/13/riddles-mahler-sixth/) from the conductor (given to us via QR code in the program as it surely wouldn’t have fit in print), “is *the* tragic symphony—the one that tells the truth all the triumphant symphonies dare not tell.”

I was left wishing I could hear the language of classical music in that way; I also wish I could remember enough high school Latin to read the inscription above the stage, which looks both imperious and preposterous at the same time: the men of Harvard bestowing upon themselves yet another blessing in their cloister. Yet the translation is affirming and elegant; the last line [reads](https://websites.harvard.edu/memhall/collections/latin-translations/):

> Moreover they who have been learned shall shine like the splendor of the firmament, and they who educate many to justice shall shine as stars for perpetual eternities.

Anyways, we saw bbno$ two days before and it was way more fun.

\*

For Emily’s birthday I grabbed general admission tickets to Canadian meme rapper [cosplay extraordinaire](https://www.instagram.com/p/DHJa4tKuedP/) bbno$, who I have been subjected to on most car rides since 2021’s “Edamame”. I’m no hater but meme rap, as a sub-class of novelty music, has a short half-life and bbno$ isn’t varied in his subject matter, even if that’s part of the joke. (I didn’t know until after the concert that he’s been throwing spaghetti at the wall since 2016 and has released *seven* albums.)

I don’t know why I’m mentioning that like it matters because the show was a fucking riot and joyful party. The second we got in line we saw multiple Miku cosplayers – including the incredible [Dunkin Donuts Miku](https://www.instagram.com/rufflebutt_cosplay/) of PAX East fame – and an audience of chilled out Millenials. Before the show there were Windows XP-themed slides reminding the crowd to wear earplugs and be careful if the show got to be too much.

It was the most relaxed crowd I’ve seen at a long time until it was time to appropriately freak out, which started with DJ tiiiiiiiiiip burning through a best of memes collection to get the crowd hyped. We’re talking [“Caramelldansen” nitecore](https://music.youtube.com/watch?v=UwTt2Ox_0AE&si=NSyXfaVEKMafoCvP),  [“World is mine”](https://music.youtube.com/watch?v=EuJ6UR_pD5s&si=Q05xADL4f8tyqTtg), the Nyan cat song, a house remix of the “Thomas the Tank Engine” theme, the list goes on. It was stupid and fun and set the tone. The opener, Jungle Bobby, went hard as hell and preached love and keeping each other safe and normally that kind of thing would annoy me but it was too damn joyful to be against.

Then the subwoofers got cranked, an insane early internet-themed graphics package was deployed, and bbno$ hobbled around on stage due to a back injury for something like 90 freaking minutes doing his best. Everyone went fucking nuts, it was awesome. “Edamame”, “1800”, “come to brazil”, “gigolo”, and more were bonkers to party to and dance with a thousand people.

The kayfabe of bbno$’s music was gone, which made it all even more fun and I’m a sucker for earnestness. Alex comes off as baffled that this is all real, that he gets to put on a big silly show filled with dick puns and the crowd is genuinely euphoric, and instead of becoming a dick he thanked the crowd profusely, shouted out his full team multiple times, and laughed at his own segues between tracks. He yelled “FUCK AI” and put on his [ode to ADD](https://youtu.be/dYIqX7ZLMyE?si=cWiXwEABI4ifGY8k) animated by 20 separate artists on the big screen.

He even got introspective; new tracks “diamonds are forever” and “why am i like this” get pretty real and the latter was a great last song for the show. In it he asks “And if I was gone / Would you really miss me?”, and I don’t need to hesitate to say yeah dude, that would suck and we would. We had a blast at the show, which would could be described as one bombastic, earnest shitpost, and it was one of my favorite concerts I’ve been to.
