I’m deeply embarrassed to admit that we only now have finally ditched our default Xfinity modem/router from **7 years ago**. Even in bridge mode, acting only as a modem, that thing was shitty, loud, and hot. It was also simply out of date and wasn’t giving us the download speeds we pay way too much for!

So this is a friendly reminder that if you don't buy your own modem and router, or you don't even periodically upgrade from what they give you, you'll pay more hundreds of dollars over time to have a horrible piece of equipment screeching in the corner of the home office making your network worse.

I picked up a simple [Hitron Coda56](https://us.hitrontech.com/products/consumers/coda56/) and it took ten minutes to set up. (Credit where credit is due, activating a new modem through Xfinity’s mobile app was easy and far better than calling and sitting on hold like the old days.) The office is markedly quieter now, our connection is future proofed with support for COAX splitting and a 2.5 gig ethernet port, and I can pay Xfinity – a company so loathed it has a Wikipedia page dedicated to its [shit reputation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Criticism_of_Comcast) – less. Win, win, win. A tiny dose of praxis against how awful America’s broadband industry is.
