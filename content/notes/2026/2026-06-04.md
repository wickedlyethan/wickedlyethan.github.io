I do miss a few things about Android after [switching](/notes/2026-05-06/). They're minor but they do show a philosophical difference, namely that iOS devices do not want to be ignored.

While Focus modes are nice they're highfalutin compared to telling your phone to shut the fuck up, but when silent mode is too much, it's annoying that I can't set Do Not Disturb in increments of time instead of 1 hour, "this evening", or by location (which is right on the line of intrusive, or at least an inconvenient reminder of what's happening in the background all the time). On Android I could set Do Not Disturb in fifteen minute increments, such as an hour and a half when I'm going into a movie.

Pixel phones also have the incredible ["Flip to Shh"](https://www.howtogeek.com/798418/how-to-mute-calls-by-flipping-your-android-phone/) feature that activates Do Not Disturb when the phone is face down, like when you're desperately trying to be social during dinner or working at a desk surrounded by Apple devices, each of which pings within seconds of each other when a message hits. I keep my iPad perpetually on silent mode and I shudder to think of a watch vibrating on my person every time my wife sends me an Instagram reel.

Combined with Apple notifications being worse – they are, it's hard to articulate but starts with the fact that on Android you can [do stuff with them](https://developer.android.com/develop/ui/compose/notifications/create-notification) – and the insistence on swiping up when unlocking your phone for [fortieth time today](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12582163/), iOS feels like I should never *not* be using it. My screen time has shot up – in part because the phone is nicer to use – and that's not the outcome I wanted.
