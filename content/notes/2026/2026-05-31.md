The *Outlander* finale was one of my favorite finales of all time. No hot take, recap blog, or forum copium can change my mind either, which is nice. A show that [means so much to me](/blog/2019/12/media-retrospective-2019/#outlander-all-4-seasons-so-far) not only stuck the landing but capitalized entirely on its central theme and genre trappings to be completely of itself and like nothing else.

(The best shows are ones that deliver moments that no other show can – other Ronald Moore joints like *Battlestar Galactica* and *For All Mankind*; the incomparable *Interview with the Vampire*; *The Crown* and its jagged mirror *The Great*; *Ted Lasso*; *Our Flag Means Death*, and so many more. You can't get this - whatever "this" is – anywhere else.)

*Outlander*'s finale was perfect because it was the *most* *Outlander* it could be, and as a bonus it leaves the whole show stronger in its context. It'll reward the inevitable rewatch, not decimate years of goodwill. That can't be taken for granted nowadays.
