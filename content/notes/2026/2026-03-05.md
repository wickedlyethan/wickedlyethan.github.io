Hayley Williams’ *Ego Death At A Bachelorette Party* (2025) has slowly become one of my favorite recent albums, and the one-two punch of “Mirtazapine” followed by “Disappearing Man” is unbeatable. Compared to her previous solo work, *Petals for Armor* (2020), which had a more claustrophobic “you’re locked in here with me” vibe, Williams’ lyrics are blunt force trauma throughout the album – writing an loud rock and roll ode to your antidepressant, for example:

> Here come my genie in a screw cap bottle\
To grant me temporary solace\
I could never be without her\
I had to write a song about her\
Who am I without you now?

Followed up by a twisty, glitch-effected ballad that ends with startlingly specificity:

> Disappearing man, that's thin as air with wild hair\
And a stare that could melt stone\
There were no conditions to my love\
Yeah, it was endless 'til there was no you to hold

It’s amazing work, though *Ego Death At A Bachelorette Party* maybe goes into the category of “could a depressed person listen to THIS multiple times a day??”
