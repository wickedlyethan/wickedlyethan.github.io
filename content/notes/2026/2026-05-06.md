I switched to iOS after using Android for **14 years**. I thought about writing a long post about switching operating systems; I thought about writing a satire of long posts about switching operating systems. I’m writing neither because the truth is reading about someone’s choice of consumer software is either riveting confirmation bias or as boring as hearing someone tell you their dream.

The only salient observation I have is that both OSes are so feature dense, with so many fiddly switches under various layers of UI strata, that I’m forced to approach switching with zen. I must accept the discomfort that I don’t know everything about iOS like I did Android, and that’s okay. It all works well enough, and what I used to think of as “mission critical” apps and functions aren't that way anymore. But hey, my bubbles are blue now.
