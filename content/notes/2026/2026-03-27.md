In another great episode of the "comedy video game news show" [Checkpoint](https://youtu.be/Uds6jGp3qbs?si=1pc6Fuhoo_uoIgAM), Graham Stark perfectly lays out the state of the industry:

> Here are your possible results as an employee of the video games industry: If you don't make a game, you lose your job. If you were making a game, but then an executive decides you have to stop making that game, you lose your job. If you do make a game, but the game isn't successful, you lose your job. If you make a game and it is successful, you lose your job. If you make a game so successful that you've warped the entire industry to follow in your footsteps, you and 999 other people still lose your jobs. I recommend you simply not do it.

Not to be hipster about it, but I made that same determination in 2018 when I graduated from school with a degree in game design, having gone to GDC that year and heard nothing but tales of horror from the attempting unionizers being ground into dust.

Of the graduates of my program, only one person seems to have had a stable career in the industry: he got a job at Epic as a QA analyst and, in an impressive move of grace and talent, brought multiple other graduates with him. He got laid off this week after five years. The number of folks working in games from that game design program can be counted on one hand.
